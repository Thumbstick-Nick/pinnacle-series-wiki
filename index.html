Peak

The property valuation & analysis engine. Peak is built for assessors and valuation professionals who need to dig deep into a property's market position, find comparables, and build defensible opinions of value.

Within Pinnacle Suite Log in to Pinnacle → click "Peak" from the module sidebar

Core Use Cases

  • Comparable Search: Find 3-5 recent arm's-length sales; refine by property type, location, and condition.
  • Market Analysis: View trends, price-per-square-foot trends, and absorption rates in your market segment.
  • Valuation Models: Build cost, market, and income approaches side-by-side. Reconcile to final opinion.
  • Appeal Prep: Export your analysis with charts, maps, and comparable details—ready to present to a judge or appeals board.

Key Features

  • Drag-and-drop adjustments: Tweak comp adjustments in real-time; see your final estimate update instantly.
  • Built-in maps: Click any property to see its footprint, neighborhood context, and sales history.
  • Confidence scoring: Peak flags weak comps so you know which analyses need more work.
  • Snapshot exports: Save your analysis as a PDF, Excel, or interactive HTML snapshot for reports and legal proceedings.

Workflow Tips

Most analysts use Peak in this order:

  1. Import: Load your subject property details (address, lot size, improvements, prior sale price).
  2. Search: Use the comparable finder to pull candidates based on your criteria.
  3. Review: Inspect each comparable's photos, tax record, and recent sales.
  4. Adjust: Build your adjustments model (size, condition, location, time, etc.).
  5. Reconcile: Compare your final estimate to the assessed value; document your reasoning.
  6. Export: Generate your report and file it with your appeal or appraisal memo.

Tip: Peak works best when you've already done your homework on market trends and recent sales. If you're new to your market, spend an hour in Market Analysis first to get a feel for what's normal.

Classic

The mass appraisal workhorse. Classic handles bulk property evaluation, large-scale data imports, formula-based modeling, and annual revaluation cycles—everything a clerical and valuation team needs to keep thousands of properties in balance.

Within Pinnacle Suite Log in to Pinnacle → click "Classic" from the module sidebar

Core Use Cases

  • Annual Revaluation: Import prior-year data, update sale prices and market indices, generate new assessed values across your jurisdiction.
  • Data Cleanup: Standardize address formats, flag missing or conflicting property details, bulk-update tax record fields.
  • Model Validation: Test your valuation formula on a sample of properties before rolling it out county-wide.
  • Exception Handling: Identify outliers, flag properties for manual review, and merge or split parcels as needed.

Key Features

  • Bulk import/export: CSV, Excel, or API feeds—load thousands of records in minutes.
  • Validation rules: Set constraints (e.g., "land value must be 20-40% of total," "no parcel can jump more than 15% in one year") and flag violations.
  • Formula editor: Build custom valuation formulas using drag-and-drop logic; test before deployment.
  • Audit trail: Track every change to every property; export reports for board meetings and legal discovery.
  • Batch operations: Reassess a whole neighborhood, apply a cost index update, or recalculate depreciation in one click.

Workflow Tips

A typical annual cycle looks like this:

  1. Import Prior Year: Load last year's assessed data as your baseline.
  2. Ingest New Sales: Feed in arm's-length transactions to recalibrate your models.
  3. Validate Data: Run your constraint rules; fix errors and outliers before modeling.
  4. Build Formula: Adjust market indices and depreciation schedules; test on a pilot subset.
  5. Roll Out: Run the formula across all properties; review exception reports.
  6. Publish: Generate tax bills and notification letters; upload to the county website.

Critical step: Always run a pilot test on 5-10% of your portfolio before going live with a new formula. This catches logic errors and unintended outliers before they affect thousands of bills.

Reports

The nerve center for insight and oversight. Reports pulls data from Peak and Classic to give assessors, administrators, and elected officials a clear view of portfolio performance, market trends, and revenue impact.

Within Pinnacle Suite Log in to Pinnacle → click "Reports" from the module sidebar

Core Reports

  • Portfolio Summary: Total assessed value, median price per square foot, cap rate trends, by property type and neighborhood.
  • Appeal Analysis: How many appeals filed, success rate, average adjustment, by property class and year.
  • Market Trends: Year-over-year price movement, sales volume, days on market, absorption rates.
  • Revenue Impact: Projected revenue from new assessments, impact of pending appeals, billable amount under cap laws.
  • Parcel Status: Count of properties in each assessment stage (pending, approved, appealed, sold); flag stuck parcels.
  • Valuation Model Performance: Compare Classic formula estimates to actual sales; identify segments that consistently over or underestimate.

Key Features

  • Interactive dashboards: Drill down by property type, location, value range, sale date, or appeal status.
  • Custom date ranges: Compare any two time periods; identify seasonal patterns and year-over-year shifts.
  • Chart exports: Download charts as PNG or SVG; use them in board presentations or media.
  • Scheduled reports: Set up daily, weekly, or monthly auto-emails to your board, auditor, or leadership team.
  • Audit-ready formatting: All charts include source data, calculation method, and confidence intervals for compliance audits.

Workflow Tips

Most leaders use Reports in three ways:

  1. Weekly Check-in: Open Portfolio Summary every Friday to spot anomalies or trends developing in real time.
  2. Monthly Board Report: Pull Revenue Impact + Appeal Analysis into a slide deck for your elected officials.
  3. Annual Audit: Export Valuation Model Performance and Appeal Analysis to demonstrate the fairness and consistency of your assessments.

Pro tip: If your board wants a custom metric, don't wait—just ask. Most "special reports" can be built in under an hour, and the engineering team is usually happy to add it permanently if other customers want it too.

AI Chat / SPARK / KnowledgeSmart

Your always-on property tax advisor. These tools use machine learning to answer routine questions, surface relevant comparable sales, and help your team work faster and smarter.

Within Pinnacle Suite Integrated in Peak and Classic; also available as a web chat on pinnacleseries.com

Core Capabilities

  • Comparable Recommendation: Describe a property, and the AI surfaces 5-10 recent arm's-length sales that match your criteria.
  • Market Insight: Ask "What are homes selling for in Downtown district?" and get instant stats, trends, and outliers.
  • Valuation Help: Get suggested adjustment amounts based on recent comparable analysis in your market.
  • Regulatory Guidance: Ask about appeal deadlines, required documentation, or state appraisal standards.
  • Case Preparation: Upload a property file; the AI summarizes comparable evidence and flags weak points in your analysis.

Key Features

  • Context-aware: The chat remembers your jurisdiction, property type preferences, and prior analyses.
  • Source citations: Every answer includes links to the comparables, regulations, or reports it used.
  • Confidence scoring: The AI tells you when it's very sure, somewhat sure, or needs more info.
  • Human handoff: If the AI can't answer, it escalates to a Pinnacle expert with one click.

Workflow Tips

Teams typically use AI Chat for:

  1. Quick lookups: "What was the last sale price for 123 Main St?"
  2. Comparable hunting: "Find 5 single-family homes sold in Q3 2024 in the downtown area, 1500-2000 sqft."
  3. Trend spotting: "Are waterfront properties appreciating faster than inland in our county?"
  4. Appeal prep: "Help me build a comp package for a 2-bedroom condo in the arts district."

Disclaimer: AI Chat is a research assistant, not a substitute for human professional judgment. Always review its suggestions against your own market knowledge and regulations.

Glossary

Assessed Value: The official value of a property for tax purposes, set by the assessor. Often abbreviated as AV.

Comparable Sale (Comp): A recent arm's-length sale of a property similar to the subject, used to estimate market value through the sales approach.

Market Value: The price at which a property would sell in a competitive, open market with reasonable time for exposure and typical buyer-seller motivation.

Sales Ratio: The ratio of assessed value to actual sale price (AV / Sale Price). In a well-calibrated jurisdiction, this ratio should be close to 1.0 and consistent across all property types and neighborhoods.

CAMA: Computer-Assisted Mass Appraisal. The methodology and software used to value large numbers of properties consistently and transparently.

Cost Approach: A valuation method that estimates value as the cost of land plus the cost to rebuild improvements, minus depreciation.

Income Approach: A valuation method that estimates value based on the income (rent, lease payments, etc.) a property can generate.

State Equalization: The state-level process that ensures all counties are assessing properties fairly relative to each other, typically by comparing sales ratios across jurisdictions.

Cap Rate (Capitalization Rate): The percentage of annual income divided by total property value. Used mainly in income-approach valuations of commercial and investment properties.

Appeal: A property owner's formal challenge to the assessed value, usually heard by a local review board or county commission.
